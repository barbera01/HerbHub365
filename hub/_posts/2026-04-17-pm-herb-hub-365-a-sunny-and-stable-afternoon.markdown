The afternoon at Herb Hub 365 brought a noticeable shift in the greenhouse atmosphere as the sun climbed higher. By early morning, the air was cool, but by midday, the environment warmed significantly.

Light levels played a major role in the day's rhythm. We started with a gentle amount of ambient light, which climbed steadily as the sun took hold. By 12:30 PM, the sensors recorded a bright reading, providing plenty of energy for photosynthesis.

Despite the warming air, the plants held their ground well, with soil moisture levels remaining robust across the board. Our basil pot was the most hydrated, while the oregano was slightly drier, yet everything appeared balanced with no warnings triggered.

It was a productive window for growth, characterized by a steady rise in temperature and light without any stress signals from the plants. As the day winds down, the data suggests the greenhouse is well-equipped to handle the midday heat while keeping the herbs happy and hydrated.
