The afternoon readings from the greenhouse on May 14th revealed a consistent pattern of growth and stability across all the herb zones. Overall ambient temperatures were pleasing, peaking near 24.66°C before gradually settling as the day progressed.

Light levels remained quite active throughout the afternoon, averaging around 681.28 lux. We observed the natural light cycle—a significant climb followed by a gentle taper—perfectly tracking the passage of time.

Soil moisture levels were stable across the board today. Basil averaged a healthy 62.51% moisture content, while the Chilli and Oregano maintained readings in the 51% to 57% range. This indicates the plants are keeping a consistent and ideal level of hydration.

Individual pot temperatures were also steady. Basil averaged a pleasant 19.33°C, Chilli at 18.91°C, and Oregano at 19°C. The water reservoir temperature averaged 18.29°C, confirming a cool and stable microclimate for all our greens as the day winds down.
