The morning at Herb Hub 365 began with a clear sky, climbing from a gentle 61 lux at dawn to a bright 377 lux by midday. Inside, the air stayed comfortably dry around 41% humidity as temperatures warmed from just under 19°C to a pleasant 20.6°C.

Our soil moisture sensors reveal distinct needs across the beds. The basil and oregano rest comfortably in the upper 50% range, while the chilli plant works hard to draw moisture, sitting at roughly 29%.

Despite the dry air, the water reservoir has not yet been touched, so the plants rely entirely on their own reserves for now. There is no cause for alarm today, though the chilli is approaching its lower threshold.

With the pressure stable and the environment warming, the greenery is thriving under these sunny conditions. We will keep an eye on reservoir levels to ensure the chilli does not run dry before the next water top-up.
