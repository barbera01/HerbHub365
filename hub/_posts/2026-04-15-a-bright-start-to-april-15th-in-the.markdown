The morning sun broke through early on April 15th, lifting the greenhouse from a gentle dawn light to bright mid-morning conditions. Inside, the air warmed steadily as the day progressed, climbing from a comfortable 22.4°C to a peak of 23.35°C. Humidity remained on the drier side, hovering around an average of 36% before rising slightly towards the end of the observation window.

Our three herbs responded well to the warming conditions, with their individual temperatures tracking just a degree or two behind the ambient air. The basil pot was the warmest at 22.4°C, while the chilli and oregano pots stayed slightly cooler at around 21.6°C. The water reservoir remained stable at roughly 20.1°C, providing a consistent base temperature for the roots.

Water management was steady throughout the day, with the chilli plant holding the most moisture at 74%. The oregano sat comfortably in the middle at 46%, while the basil required a bit more attention as its moisture level dipped slightly from 32.45% to 31.33% as the sun intensified. No warnings were triggered, and the system continued to monitor everything smoothly.
