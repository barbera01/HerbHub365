The sun shone brightly over the greenhouse this afternoon, with light levels rising from a quiet morning peak until midday. Lux readings climbed past eight hundred at their highest point before settling back down as the day progressed. Inside remained comfortable for our plants, warming gradually to reach 23.68°C by late in the day.

Soil moisture tells a different story across the pots. Basil and oregano sit comfortably with healthy hydration, though basil has drawn slightly from yesterday's higher start. The chilli plant needs attention soon; its soil dropped significantly over these hours, falling below thirty-five percent by our observation window closed.

Humidity hovered around forty-four percent on average, dipping as the sun climbed before stabilizing again later. Pressure readings were steady throughout, indicating calm weather outside. While there are no critical warnings flashing red today, that dropping moisture level in the chilli pot is something to keep an eye on until tomorrow's update arrives.
