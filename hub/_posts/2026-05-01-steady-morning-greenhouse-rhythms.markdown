It was a calm start at Herb Hub 365 this morning. Between 06:00 and 08:30 UTC, our sensors recorded a gentle rise in ambient temperature from 20.33°C to 21.49°C. This steady warmth provided a stable, inviting environment for the herbs to begin their day.

As the sun climbed, light levels increased from 130 to 241.7 lux. This brightening was paired with a slight dip in humidity, which moved from 38.2% down to 37.1%. Meanwhile, atmospheric pressure held firm at 1017 hPa, signaling consistent, clear weather conditions outside the glass.

Soil moisture levels showed a typical morning decline as the plants began their daily activity. The Oregano remains the most hydrated at 50.14%, followed by the Chilli at 44.53% and the Basil at 40.59%. While all three are within healthy ranges, we are monitoring the Basil closely as it remains the driest of the trio.

The plants themselves stayed remarkably cool, with internal temperatures for the Basil, Chilli, and Oregano all hovering around 20°C. Though specific water reservoir data was unavailable for this window, the steady soil moisture indicates a well-balanced system. It is a peaceful and productive start to the month of May.
