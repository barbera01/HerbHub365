The morning sun has fully broken over the Herb Hub 365 greenhouse, bringing a noticeable rise in both ambient and plant temperatures. We started the day at a gentle 18.48°C, but by mid-afternoon, the air warmed to a comfortable 23.21°C. Our basil, chilli, and oregano plants tracked closely with these shifts, settling into a cozy range between 17.9°C and 21.1°C.

Light levels climbed steadily throughout the observation window, starting at a dim 65 lux in the early morning and reaching a bright 456.7 lux by the final reading. This increase in illumination drove a corresponding drop in humidity, which fell from 44.2% down to 31.1% as the day progressed. The air is becoming drier, but the conditions remain well within the healthy range for our growing herbs.

Soil moisture levels are holding steady across the board. The basil and oregano pots remain comfortably hydrated, hovering between 47.6% and 49.5% moisture. The chilli plants, which naturally require less water, sit at a lower but stable 25.9% to 26.8%. There are no warnings from the sensors, and the water reservoir is maintaining a consistent temperature around 17.5°C, ensuring a stable root environment for everyone.
