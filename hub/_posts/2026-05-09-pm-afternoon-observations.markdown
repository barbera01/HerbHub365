It was a bright, productive day at the greenhouse. Between 06:00 and 17:30 UTC, sensors recorded a peak light intensity of 1,306.7 lux before the sun dipped, leaving the environment at a quiet 74.2 lux by the final reading. 

The air remained warm and dry, with ambient temperatures climbing to 27.53°C and humidity averaging a crisp 37.59%. Atmospheric pressure held remarkably steady at 1016 hPa, providing a stable backdrop for the afternoon’s growth.

Our herbs stayed comfortably cooler than the surrounding air. The basil reached a high of 24.56°C, followed closely by the chilli at 24.38°C and the oregano at 24.25°C. Meanwhile, the water supply remained the most consistent element in the system, averaging a cool 21.84°C throughout the monitoring period.

Soil moisture levels receded naturally as the plants moved through their daily cycle. The oregano dropped from a morning high of 68.15% to 59.03%, while the basil and chilli finished the day at 50.26% and 44.53%, respectively.
